The ADHD Dopamine Menu: How to Get a Focus Boost Without the Digital Crash

If you have ADHD, you know the feeling: you‚Äôre bored, under-stimulated, and your brain is screaming for a "hit" of something interesting. Usually, we reach for our phones. We start scrolling TikTok or Instagram, and before we know it, two hours have passed.

The problem? This is "Junk Dopamine." It gives you a quick spike, but it also raises your Digital Cortisol, leaving you feeling more stressed and exhausted than when you started.

The solution is a Dopamine Menu (or "Dopa-menu"). It‚Äôs a pre-made list of healthy stimulation that keeps your brain happy without the crash.

Section 1: The "Appetizers" (Quick 5-Minute Wins)

These are for when you have a tiny break or feel a "boredom itch" starting. They provide a quick boost without sucking you into a 2-hour rabbit hole.

  • Physical: 10 jumping jacks or a quick stretch.
  • Sensory: Drinking a glass of ice-cold water.
  • Environment: Opening a window for fresh air.

Section 2: The "Mains" (Deep Stimulation)

These are activities that take 20-30 minutes and actually "recharge" your brain. These are the best for lowering stress levels.

  • Creative: Sketching or journaling for 15 minutes.
  • Interactive: Playing a low-stimulus focus game (like our Digital Cortisol Reset).
  • Physical: Taking a walk without headphones.
